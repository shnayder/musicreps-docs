## **Brand Guide**

### **1\. Core Positioning**

**Tagline**

**Make music fundamentals automatic so you can focus on making music.**

Core idea:
The app trains **instant recall of music fundamentals** through many efficient reps.

It is not primarily about learning concepts. It is about moving from **knowing → automatic knowing**.

Mental model:

**Strength training for music knowledge.**
Q: How much to lean into strength training specifically vs general mastery-through-sustained-effort.

---

## **2\. Core Promise**

Automatic fundamentals → musical freedom

The product helps musicians:

* turn music fundamentals into **reflex**
* practice efficiently with **many fast reps**
* train even when **away from their instrument**
* build **real musicianship through fundamentals**

### **Secondary Benefits**

These show *why automatic fundamentals matter*.

They should appear in screenshots, marketing sections, and onboarding.

Examples:

**Play with confidence**  
No second-guessing notes, intervals, or chords.

**Improvise more freely**  
Your brain isn’t searching for theory.

**Learn songs faster**  
Recognize patterns instantly.

**Write better music**  
Your musical vocabulary becomes intuitive.

**Understand what you hear**  
Music makes sense immediately.

**Communicate with other musicians**  
Speak the shared language of music.

---

## **3\. Supporting Value Propositions**

### **A. Automaticity**

The main differentiator.

Focus:

* instant recall
* second-nature knowledge
* no thinking required

Messaging examples:

* “Train fundamentals until they’re automatic.”
* “Build instant recall for music knowledge.”

---

### **B. Efficient Practice**

The product is optimized for **high rep counts**.

Principles:

* fast drills
* minimal friction
* spaced repetition
* no unnecessary animations or distractions

Messaging:

* “Fast drills designed for many reps.”
* “Practice exactly what you need.”

---

### **C. Practice Anywhere**

The app fills the gap when the instrument isn’t available.

Examples:

* commuting
* waiting
* spare minutes

Messaging:

* “Turn spare minutes into real practice.”
* “Train music fundamentals anywhere.”

---

### **D. Mastery Mindset**

The philosophy behind the product.

Progress comes from:

* mastering the basics
* consistent reps
* long-term musicianship

Messaging examples:

* “Master the basics.”
* “Daily reps for real musicianship.”

This philosophy informs tone but should not dominate the main headline.


---

## **4\. Positioning Relative to Other Apps**

### **Duolingo**

Positioning:

* gamified lessons

Contrast:

* fewer animations
* less gamification
* more focused reps

---

### **Coursera / online courses**

Positioning:

* structured instruction

Contrast:

* not a course
* not lectures
* pure practice

---

### **Simply Piano / Yousician**

Positioning:

* guided playing with feedback

Contrast:

* not instrument feedback
* trains mental knowledge

---

### **Anki**

Positioning:

* spaced repetition flashcards

Overlap:

* efficient memory training

Difference:

* domain-specific for musicians
* designed for **rapid reps**
* focused on **musical fundamentals**

---

### **Tenuto / theory reference apps**

Positioning:

* theory exercises or reference

Difference:

* strong emphasis on **automatic recall**
* optimized for **high-rep training**

---

## **5\. Visual and Icon Design**

### **Visual concept**

**Training / reps**

The product should visually feel like **practice**, not lessons.

Good cues:

* repetition
* strength or other training
* focused effort

---

### **Icon direction**

**music knowledge through repetition**

Examples:

* repetition marks
* bold music symbol with training motif
* music + strength training

Requirements:

* strong silhouette
* minimal detail
* recognizable at small size

(Going with "repeat" symbol for now.)

---

### **Color and tone**

Design should feel:

* focused
* serious
* minimal

Suggested palette (TBD):

* dark neutral background
* one strong accent color

Avoid:

* playful learning-app aesthetics
* overly bright or childish palettes

---

## **6\. UI Design Principles**

The interface should feel like **doing reps**.

Design implications:

* instant transitions
* minimal animation
* quick answers
* rapid progression to the next question
* emphasize effort as much as skill level -- rep counts, streaks. If you keep working, good things will happen. 

The experience should feel like:

**training**, not browsing or studying.

---

## **7\. Marketing Structure**

All marketing should reinforce the same narrative.

### **Hero message**

Make music fundamentals automatic so you can focus on playing.

---

### **Supporting sections**

**Fast drills**

* Train fundamentals with many quick reps.

**Practice anywhere**

* Use spare moments away from your instrument.

**Master the basics**

* Build real musicianship through fundamentals.

---

## **8\. Messaging Principles**

When choosing wording:

Prefer:

* automatic
* second nature
* fundamentals
* playing
* reps
* drills

Avoid emphasizing:

* “learning theory”
* “courses”
* “tips and tricks”

The product promise is **automatic recall**, not instruction.

---

## **9\. Consistency Across the Product**

The same idea should appear in:

* app icon
* App Store subtitle
* screenshots
* onboarding text
* UI interaction style
* marketing site
* push notifications

Everything should reinforce:

**Reps → automatic fundamentals → better playing.**
