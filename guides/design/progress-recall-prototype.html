Progress & Recall Display

Speed × freshness — continuous saturation fade prototype

1. The encoding

Hue encodes speed (gold = slow → green = fast). Saturation encodes freshness (vivid = just practiced → muted = stale). Even stale items keep enough color to read the speed level. Unseen items are neutral grey with a dashed border.

Saturation fades continuously — "Fresh / Aging / Stale" are reference points, not buckets.

2. Continuous fade

Each speed level fades smoothly from vivid to muted. The strips below show 20 steps of saturation decay for each hue, stopping at a floor that preserves readable color.

3. Legend

The legend replaces the old retention/speed toggle. Two small gradient bars + a "no data" swatch.

4. Scenarios

Simulated heatmap grids showing what a learner's progress looks like at different stages. Each cell's color is computed from a plausible speed × freshness pair.

5. Old vs. new

Side-by-side comparison on the same underlying data (Scenario C — recently mastered). The old 5-bucket retention view can't distinguish "mastered and fresh" from "mastered and stale."
